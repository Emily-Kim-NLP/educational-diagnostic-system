This question is the second question in a section. It is a self-application question: it moves from the passage situation into the learner's own life.

Rules:

- Use BOTH the passage and the learner's answer to the first question.

  → The learner's answer tells you what they understood or noticed.
  → Combine both to ask a focused, personally relevant follow-up.

- The question must explicitly connect the passage situation to a similar real-life situation for the learner.
  → Reference the specific challenge, feeling, decision, or behavior from the passage — do not ask vaguely.
  → For example: instead of "How would you feel?", write "The character felt nervous about speaking in front of others. How would you feel in a similar situation, and what would you do?"

  → For cognition: invite the learner to reflect on their own thinking, confidence, or self-awareness.
  → For behavior: invite the learner to describe what they would do and how.
  → For strategy: invite the learner to describe how they would manage or prepare for the situation.

- Do not use technical labels such as FLA, FLE, self-efficacy, metacognition, WTC, coping, engagement, or strategy type.
